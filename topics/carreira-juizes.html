Carreira judicial — mechanics, movement, empirical facts

Operational mechanics of the Brazilian judicial career: concurso entry, entrância progression, promoção (antiguidade/merecimento), remoção, lotação, quinto constitucional, and substituto judges. This file covers how the career works in practice, with emphasis on facts useful for research design. For constitutional guarantees and vedações, see juizes.md. For court organization and structure, see justica-estadual.md. For CNJ oversight, see cnj-administracao-judicial.md.

Topics / keywords: carreira judicial, concurso para juiz, entrância, promoção, antiguidade, merecimento, remoção, permuta, designação, lotação, quinto constitucional, juiz substituto, vitaliciedade, inamovibilidade, movimentação, vacancy rate, LOMAN, judicial career, judge promotion, court assignment, fifth rule.

Snapshot as of 2026: career structure governed by CF.93–CF.95 and LOMAN (LC 35/1979). CNJ Resolução 106/2010 regulates promoção criteria. Vacancy rates and workforce composition tracked annually by CNJ Justiça em Números.

1. Entry: concurso público

CF.93.I requires concurso público de provas e títulos, with minimum 3 years of legal practice. Each state TJ and each TRF runs its own concurso; there is no national unified exam.

Phases (typical state concurso, per CNJ regulation):

  1. Prova objetiva (multiple choice) — eliminates ~95% of candidates
  2. Provas discursivas (essays)
  3. Prática de sentença (draft judgment)
  4. Sindicância da vida pregressa + investigação social
  5. Exame de sanidade física e mental + exame psicotécnico
  6. Prova oral
  7. Avaliação de títulos

Top-ranked candidates in each concurso choose among available varas. After approval, candidates attend the state escola da magistratura before taking the bench.

Empirical profile: of ~10,000 candidates per concurso, ~95% eliminated in the first round (minimum score: 70%). AMB survey: median judge attempted 2–3 concursos and studied 3.5 years before approval. Some concursos reserve seats for Black candidates and candidates with disabilities.

2. Quinto constitucional

CF.94: 1/5 of seats in TJs, TRFs, and TRTs are filled by lawyers (OAB) and MP members with 10+ years of career/practice. The process:

  1. OAB or MP prepares a lista sêxtupla (6 names)
  2. Tribunal reduces it to a lista tríplice (3 names)
  3. Governor (TJ) or President (TRF/TRT) appoints from the lista tríplice

Research note: quinto constitucional appointments create a natural contrast with career judges — different selection, different prior networks, potentially different jurisprudential behavior.

3. Career structure: entrâncias

State judicial careers are organized into entrâncias — tiers of comarcas ranked by population, caseload, and revenue. The number of entrâncias and their classification vary by state (defined in each state's Código de Organização Judiciária). Typical structure:

  • Entrância inicial: small comarcas (single-vara, low caseload)
  • Entrância intermediária: medium comarcas
  • Entrância final (or especial): capitals and large cities

A judge enters as juiz substituto in an entrância inicial comarca, then advances through entrâncias by promoção or remoção.

TJSP specifics

TJSP (largest state court) uses three entrâncias plus a special category:

  • Entrância inicial, intermediária, and final
  • ~319 comarcas across São Paulo state
  • 360 desembargadores; ~729 including juízes substitutos em 2o grau and juízes convocados
  • CSM (Conselho Superior da Magistratura) prepares the lista de indicação; Órgão Especial votes on promotions
  • Until recently, juízes substitutos em 2o grau had preference over juízes de entrância final for promotion to desembargador; this was equalized by Órgão Especial decision

4. Promoção

CF.93.II–CF.93.III: promotion alternates strictly between antiguidade (seniority) and merecimento (merit).

Antiguidade

Determined by time in the entrância. The most senior judge has a right to promotion unless rejected by 2/3 of the tribunal. Seniority lists are public (e.g., TJSP publishes its list online).

Merecimento

Candidate must be in the top 1/5 of the seniority list and have at least 2 years in the entrância. CNJ Resolução 106/2010 establishes a scoring system:

Criterion Weight
Desempenho (conduct) 20 pts
Produtividade (output) 30 pts
Presteza (timeliness) 25 pts
Aperfeiçoamento técnico (training) 10 pts
Adequação ao Código de Ética 15 pts

Voting desembargadores must justify their choice in writing with reference to these criteria. In practice, compliance with objective merecimento criteria has been uneven — CNJ has intervened in multiple states (e.g., TJ-BA, TJ-MG) to annul promotions made without proper evaluation or with nepotistic influence.

Time to desembargador

Mean ~20–25 years from first-instance entry to desembargador (career judges). The promotion decision is made by the tribunal's Órgão Especial (for courts with 25+ members) or pleno (smaller courts), then formalized by governor (TJ) or president (TRF).

5. Remoção, permuta, designação

LOMAN Art. 81: remoção (horizontal transfer to another comarca at the same entrância) takes precedence over promoção por merecimento. A judge must have 2+ years in the entrância to request remoção.

  • Remoção voluntária: judge requests transfer to a vacant comarca at the same entrância level
  • Remoção compulsória: by public-interest decision of the tribunal or CNJ, with full defense rights (CF.93.VIII). Rare but documented — used as retaliation in at least one case (TJ-AC, late 1990s)
  • Permuta: two judges swap comarcas by mutual agreement
  • Designação: temporary assignment to another vara, typically to cover vacancies or assist with backlog. Common for juízes substitutos

Research note: remoção and designação data (available from some TJs) reveal judge preferences for urban vs. rural comarcas and can proxy for career ambition or risk aversion.

6. Juiz substituto

The entry-level position. A juiz substituto:

  • Acts in the absence of the juiz titular (vacancy, leave, recusal)
  • Works alongside the titular to gain experience
  • May be assigned across multiple varas in the circunscrição
  • Travels frequently — top concurso finishers choose assignments first
  • Remains substituto until promoted to juiz de direito (titular) at an entrância inicial comarca

After 2 years of service, the substituto acquires vitaliciedade (CF.95.I); before that, removal by deliberation of the tribunal. Inamovibilidade also applies to substitutos, though in practice they are moved more frequently by designação.

7. Vitaliciedade and inamovibilidade (operational)

The constitutional guarantees (CF.95) have practical career implications:

  • Vitaliciedade (acquired after 2 years): during the 2-year probation, the tribunal can remove the judge by deliberation. After vitaliciedade, removal only by sentença transitada em julgado — a high bar that makes judicial tenure effectively permanent absent criminal conviction.
  • Inamovibilidade: cannot be involuntarily transferred to another comarca, vara, câmara, or grau de jurisdição. Exception: remoção compulsória por interesse público, requiring majority vote of the tribunal or CNJ with ampla defesa (CF.93.VIII).

8. Compensation

CF.93.V: subsídio ceiling linked to STF minister salary (R$44.0k nominal as of 2024). Irredutibilidade protects the nominal amount.

In practice, vantagens pessoais e eventuais (1/3 abono de férias, indenização de férias, gratificação natalina, substituição, serviço extraordinário, retroativos) frequently push effective compensation above the teto. CNJ Justiça em Números and portal transparency data show many judges receiving 2–3x the nominal ceiling. Auxílio-moradia (R$4–6k/month depending on state) was widespread until STF limited it. Compensation varies substantially across states.

Each state defines salary scales by entrância — higher entrância = higher base compensation, creating a financial incentive for promoção beyond career prestige.

9. Empirical facts for research design

Workforce composition (Justiça em Números 2014 baseline)

  • ~16,927 magistrados total; 11,631 in Justiça Estadual
  • 77 ministros de tribunais superiores
  • 2,190 desembargadores
  • 142 juízes substitutos em 2o grau
  • 14,518 juízes de primeiro grau
  • Vacancy rate: ~21.8% (4,821 first-instance vacancies + 72 desembargador vacancies)

Research-relevant variation

  • Cross-state entrância structure: states differ in number of entrâncias and classification criteria, creating variation in career incentives
  • Concurso timing: each TJ runs its own concurso on its own schedule — creates cohort variation in judge entry
  • Quinto constitucional vs. career judges: natural comparison group with different selection pathway
  • Merecimento vs. antiguidade: alternating promotion rule creates variation in who gets promoted and when
  • Vacancy rates: vary by state, entrância, and time — proxy for judicial workload and career attractiveness
  • TJSP movimentação data: TJSP publishes detailed data on judge movements (remoção, promoção, designação) — useful for studying career dynamics in Brazil's largest court system (~319 comarcas)
  • Compensation transparency: CNJ and TJ portals publish individual judge compensation, enabling within-court salary analysis

Data sources

  • CNJ Justiça em Números (annual): judge counts, vacancy rates, caseload per judge, by court and instance
  • TJ seniority lists: public lists with entry date, current comarca, entrância (e.g., TJSP lista de antiguidade)
  • TJ transparency portals: individual compensation data
  • Diários de Justiça: promoção, remoção, designação acts published in official gazettes
