# Polícias estaduais — civil, militar, e guarda municipal

State-level public-security institutions under `CF.144`. Covers
Polícia Civil (state judicial police), Polícia Militar (ostensive
policing), and Guardas Municipais. Operational parallels to the
Polícia Federal but subordinated to state governors, not the União.
Relevant for accountability research because state-level criminal
corruption flows through Polícia Civil → MPE, paralleling the
PF → MPF federal pipeline.

For PF, see `policia-federal.md`. For MP side (including MPE controle
externo), see `ministerio-publico.md`. For state criminal courts,
see `justica-estadual.md`. For the municipal-corruption pipeline,
see `fluxo-corrupcao-municipal.md` §4.

**Topics / keywords**: Polícia Civil, PC, polícia judiciária estadual,
delegado de polícia, Polícia Militar, PM, policiamento ostensivo,
inquérito policial, Justiça Militar Estadual, Guarda Municipal, GM,
GCM, polícia penal, corregedoria, controle externo da atividade
policial, CF Art. 144.

**Snapshot as of 2026**: constitutional structure (`CF.144`) is
stable. `EC104-2019` created the **Polícia Penal** (federal, state,
distrital) as a fourth civilian branch, replacing the former agentes
penitenciários in prison management (`CF.144.VI` — not covered in
detail here).

## 1. Polícia Civil

### Constitutional basis

- `CF.144.IV`: polícias civis são órgão da segurança pública.
- `CF.144.§4`: "Às polícias civis, dirigidas por delegados de polícia
  de carreira, incumbem, ressalvada a competência da União, as
  funções de polícia judiciária e a apuração de infrações penais,
  exceto as militares."
- `CF.144.§6`: subordination to the state governor (jointly with PM).

### Function

Polícia Civil is the **state judicial police**: it investigates
common crimes under state jurisdiction (the bulk of homicides,
robberies, furtos, estelionato, and crimes contra a administração
pública estadual/municipal). Its investigative instrument is the
inquérito policial (`CPP.4`–`CPP.23`), procedurally identical to the
PF's.

Jurisdictional division from PF:

- **Polícia Civil**: state-level crimes. Includes corruption by
  state/municipal officials when no federal nexus exists.
- **Polícia Federal**: federal interests, interstate repercussion,
  federal funds, and crimes under Lei 10.446/2002 (see
  `policia-federal.md` §1).

Many municipal corruption cases — particularly those involving
state/municipal funds without federal transfer — are investigated by
PC rather than PF. The jurisdictional line is contested in practice
(federalization via `CF.109` is limited).

### Organization

- **One PC per state**, headed by a **Delegado-Geral** appointed by
  the governor (typically a career delegado, though the formal
  requirement varies by state).
- **Delegacias** — territorial units (delegacias distritais,
  delegacias especializadas) that hold cases and conduct inquéritos.
- **Delegacias especializadas**: DP do Patrimônio Público (DPP,
  anticorrupção estadual), DEIC (investigações criminais), DPCA
  (children/adolescents), DEAM (women), others vary by state.

### Career

- **Delegado de Polícia Civil** — heads investigations; law degree
  required; state concurso. Constitutional status (`CF.144.§4`:
  "delegados de polícia de carreira") elevated the office
  symbolically but does not confer MP-style guarantees
  (vitaliciedade, etc.).
- **Investigador, Escrivão, Perito Criminal, Papiloscopista** —
  operational careers; higher-education requirements; state concursos.

### Relationship with MPE

- **Controle externo** by MPE (`CF.129.VII`; CNMP Resolução 20/2007,
  state analogues).
- **Requisição** of diligências and instauração de inquérito policial
  by MPE (`CF.129.VIII`).
- **PIC (MPE)**: since STF `RE593727` (Tema 184, 2015), MPE can also
  conduct criminal investigations directly, not only MPF. CNMP
  Resolução 181/2017 applies to all MPs. Creates a partial substitute
  for PC investigation in the same way PIC does at federal level for
  PF.

### Accountability concerns

- **Politicization**: PC is directly subordinate to the governor;
  delegado-geral rotation follows political cycles.
- **Resource constraints**: most PCs are underfunded relative to PF;
  investigative backlogs are severe in large states.
- **Internal corruption**: corregedorias da PC handle internal
  affairs; external visibility is limited. Periodic scandals
  (e.g., São Paulo's delegado involvement in crimes investigated by
  GAECO).

## 2. Polícia Militar

### Constitutional basis

- `CF.144.V`: polícias militares são órgão da segurança pública.
- `CF.144.§5`: "Às polícias militares cabem a polícia ostensiva e a
  preservação da ordem pública".
- `CF.144.§6`: PMs são "forças auxiliares e reserva do Exército";
  subordinate to state governors.

### Function

PM performs **ostensive/preventive policing** — patrol, traffic
enforcement, crowd control, response to flagrancias, riot control.
PM does **not** conduct criminal investigation (which is PC's
monopoly at state level under `CF.144.§4`). PM lavra termos
circunstanciados in minor cases and conducts prisão em flagrante,
but the subsequent inquérito is PC's responsibility.

### Organization

- **One PM per state**, organized as a military hierarchy
  (comandante-geral coronel PM at top; officers and praças below).
- **Batalhões, companhias, pelotões** — military territorial units.
- **Corpos de Bombeiros Militares** — fire brigade; in most states
  a separate corporation, but constitutionally grouped with PM
  (`CF.144.V`).

### Career

- **Oficiais**: Academia Militar do Estado; 4-year course.
- **Praças**: concurso para soldado PM; promotions through
  hierarchical ranks.
- Military discipline (`CP Militar`) applies internally.

### Relevance for corruption research

PM's direct relevance to corruption enforcement is **limited**: it
does not investigate crimes, it does not control contracts or
procurement at scale, and its daily operations do not generate the
kind of accountability flow that PC or PF produce. Peripheral
relevance via:

- **Military crimes committed by PM officers**: tried in Justiça
  Militar Estadual (`CF.125.§4`, `CF.125.§5`); ordinary crimes by PM
  (including homicídio doloso) go to Júri popular post-Lei
  9.299/1996.
- **PM involvement in corruption schemes**: occasionally as
  participants (extortion rackets, protection of illicit markets).
- **Internal administration**: PM budgets and contracts are
  audited by TCE and can appear in the municipal corruption flow.

## 3. Guardas Municipais

### Constitutional basis

- `CF.144.§8`: "Os Municípios poderão constituir guardas municipais
  destinadas à proteção de seus bens, serviços e instalações,
  conforme dispuser a lei".
- **Estatuto Geral das Guardas Municipais**: Lei 13.022/2014.

### Function

GMs protect **municipal patrimônio** (prédios, praças, escolas,
hospitais municipais). Lei 13.022/2014 expanded competences to
include traffic, school patrol, and community policing. Some large-
city GMs (São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro) function nearly as a third
police force.

### Limitations

- **No investigative power**: GMs cannot open inquérito policial
  (monopoly of PC/PF).
- **Limited arrest powers**: flagrante delito only, same as any
  citizen.
- **Accountability**: subordinate to the mayor; no constitutional
  guarantees analogous to police careers. Internal affairs via
  municipal corregedoria.

GMs are rarely central to corruption research.

## 4. Cross-institutional flow at the state level

Parallel structure to the federal-level PF–MPF–Justiça Federal
triangle (see `policia-federal.md` §4):

- PC conducts inquérito → MPE oversees → MPE files denúncia → TJ
  (criminal vara) tries.
- MPE can deploy PIC directly for criminal investigation (post-Tema
  184).
- TJ authorizes intrusive measures (interceptação, quebra de sigilo)
  on MPE or PC request.

Friction points:

- PC–MPE conflicts over investigative autonomy (same dynamic as
  PF–MPF debates).
- Political subordination of PC to the governor is sharper than
  PF–União subordination; state-level investigations of sitting
  governors face obvious conflict-of-interest concerns.
- Cross-state cooperation is weak; crimes that cross state lines
  often default to PF jurisdiction (`CF.144.§1.I`), reducing PC's
  reach for complex schemes.

|---|---|
