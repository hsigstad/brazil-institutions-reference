# Processo eleitoral

Brazilian electoral procedure: the actions, recursos, and procedural
framework that operate before the electoral courts. This file is
organized around (1) campaign accounting (*prestação de contas*),
(2) representações for misconduct, (3) the main electoral actions
(AIPRC, AIJE, AIME, RCED, Ação Penal), and (4) recursos.

For the **electoral court system itself** (TSE, TREs, juízes
eleitorais), see [`justica-eleitoral.md`](justica-eleitoral.md). For
the **substantive electoral system** (open-list PR, coligações,
campaign finance, Ficha Limpa eligibility), see
[`partidos-e-sistema-eleitoral.md`](partidos-e-sistema-eleitoral.md).
For **campaign-finance filing detail**, see
[`prestacao-contas-eleitorais.md`](prestacao-contas-eleitorais.md).
For **STF jurisprudence cited here**, see
[`../jurisprudencia-stf.md`](../jurisprudencia-stf.md).

**Topics / keywords**: processo eleitoral, electoral procedure,
prestação de contas, representação, AIPRC, AIME, AIJE, RCED,
ação penal eleitoral, captação ilícita de sufrágio, conduta vedada,
abuso de poder econômico, propaganda irregular, pedido de direito de
resposta, recurso eleitoral, recurso especial eleitoral, recurso
ordinário eleitoral, embargos de declaração, agravo interno, agravo
de instrumento, ação rescisória eleitoral, cassação, inelegibilidade.

**Snapshot as of 2026**: post-2017 reforms (EC 97/2017, fim das
coligações; Lei 13.488/2017, FEFC) reshape several procedures.
References below cite Lei das Eleições (LE = Lei 9.504/1997), Código
Eleitoral (CE = Lei 4.737/1965), and Lei das Inelegibilidades
(LC 64/1990) as in force at the time of writing.

## 2. Prestação de contas (electoral accounting)

Administrative-natured procedure for review of campaign accounts by
the electoral courts. Detail of the filing mechanics is in
[`prestacao-contas-eleitorais.md`](prestacao-contas-eleitorais.md).

### Legal basis

- Candidates: LE Art. 28 §§1–2.
- Parties: Lei dos Partidos Arts. 33 II, 34 I/V.
- Subsidiary application of Lei 8.443/1992 (LO TCU) practice.

### Filing deadlines

- **Contas parciais** (interim): cash receipts must be reported within
  72 hours via SPCE (LE Art. 28 §4 I).
- **Contas finais** (final): 30 days after the election. Elected
  candidates cannot be diplomados without their accounts having been
  julgadas (LE Art. 29 §2).

### Procedural sequence

1. **Apresentação** of accounts via SPCE (Sistema de Prestação de
   Contas Eleitorais). Simplified form available for max R$20,000 or
   for prefeito/vereador in municipalities under 50,000 voters
   (LE Art. 28 §§9–11, added 2015).
2. **Edital** opens after regular submission of final accounts.
3. **Impugnação**: 3-day window in which parties, coligações,
   candidates, the MP eleitoral, or any interested third party may
   challenge.
4. **Requisição de informações**: additional information may be
   requested ex officio.
5. **Diligências** (LE Art. 30 §4), including *circularização* —
   contacting suppliers and donors to verify reported information.
6. **Parecer técnico** by the technical body of the electoral court.
7. **Manifestação do candidato/partido**: 3 days to respond if the
   parecer indicates rejection or qualified approval.
8. **Pronunciamento técnico** revisited.
9. **Parecer do MP** (2-day deadline).
10. **Decisão**. Accounts of elected candidates must be julgadas at
    least 8 days before diplomação.
11. **Publicação**: within 3 days before diplomação (LE Art. 30 §1).
12. **Recurso eleitoral**: 3 days (CE Art. 265). No appeal to the
    superior tribunals on this specific procedure.

### Possible decisions and effects

| Decision | Party effect | Candidate effect |
|---|---|---|
| Aprovação | None | None |
| Aprovação com ressalvas | Subject to *proporcionalidade e razoabilidade* principles | None |
| Desaprovação | Loss of fundo partidário (LE Art. 25) | No automatic sanction; may trigger AIJE |
| Não prestação | Loss of fundo partidário | Impediment to *quitação eleitoral* (LE Art. 11 §7) |

- Formal/material errors that have been corrected do not authorize
  desaprovação (LE Art. 30 §2).
- Since 2010, even desaprovação generates *quitação eleitoral*; the
  practical sanction on the candidate is limited unless the MP
  pursues an AIJE.

### Post-election obligations

- **Sobras de campanha**: surplus funds must be transferred to the
  party organ (LE Art. 31) and used for institutional purposes.
- **Assunção de dívida pelo partido**: parties may continue raising
  funds after the election to settle outstanding campaign debts up
  to the prestação de contas (LE Art. 29 §§3–4). Failure to settle
  results in desaprovação.
- **Conservação de documentos**: at least 180 days after diplomação
  (LE Art. 32). Suppression of documents triggers CP Art. 305.

## 3. Representações (misconduct procedures)

The general representação for violations of the LE is governed by
LE Art. 96. Specific types follow.

### 3.1 Conduta vedada — LE Art. 73

Prohibited conduct by public agents during electoral periods (use of
public goods/services, advertising, hires, etc.).

- **Rito**: LC 64/1990 Art. 22, but **not** distributed to the juiz
  corregedor.
- **Prazo**: until diplomação (LE Art. 73 §11).
- **Prova**: the conduct does not need to have actually affected the
  electoral result (TSE Ac. 758/2004).
- **Efeito**: only multa for cases of low gravity; cassação possible
  for grave cases.

### 3.2 Captação ilícita de sufrágio — LE Art. 41-A

*Vote-buying / electoral corruption.*

- **Polo passivo**: the buyer + the candidate who benefited.
- **Rito**: LC 64/1990 Art. 22 (not distributed to the juiz
  corregedor).
- **Prova**:
  - Must be robust.
  - Need not explicitly establish the *escambo* (exchange).
  - Single witness testimony alone is not accepted (CE Art. 368-A,
    2015).
  - Recordings by one of the participants are admissible (STF RE
    583937 QO-RG, 2009); recordings by third parties require prior
    judicial authorization.
- **Conexão**:
  - Civil and criminal proceedings on the same facts run independently
    — no vinculação between cível and criminal.
  - Two separate representações for related conduct are possible, but
    AIJE often groups them.
  - Vote-buying may also be evidence of *abuso de poder econômico*
    when scale or value is significant.
- **Efeito**:
  - Cassação.
  - Multa: 1,500 to 50,000 UFIR.
  - 8 years *inelegibilidade*.
- **Caveat**: candidates who lose sometimes use the AIJE/representação
  apparatus as a "second round" — courts have noted patterns of
  induced false testimony.

### 3.3 Propaganda intrapartidária desvirtuada — LE Art. 36 §1

Pre-candidate propaganda that goes beyond seeking the party
indication and addresses the general public, or is anticipated.

- **Prazo**: until election day.
- **Competência**: juiz eleitoral (municipais), TRE (estaduais),
  TSE (federais).
- **Polo ativo**: MP eleitoral.
- **Rito**: LE Art. 96.
- **Efeito**: cessation of the propaganda, removal from the location,
  restoration, and multa.

### 3.4 Propaganda antecipada ou irregular — LE Art. 37

Propaganda before the legal start of the campaign period (currently
15 August of the election year) or in violation of the LE limits.

- **Prazo**: until election day.
- **Competência**: juiz eleitoral / TRE / TSE per office level.
- **Polo ativo**: MP, party (if not coligado), coligação, candidate.
- **Polo passivo**: the responsible party + the beneficiary.

### 3.5 Captação ou gastos ilícitos de recursos — LE Art. 30-A

Illicit financing or expenditure of campaign resources.

- **Prazo**: 15 days after diplomação.
- **Competência**: juiz eleitoral / TRE / TSE per office level.
- **Polo ativo**: MP, party, coligação.
- **Efeito**: cassação; 8 years inelegibilidade if cassação is imposed.

### 3.6 Doação acima do limite — LE Art. 23 §1

Campaign donations exceeding the legal individual limit.

- **Prazo**: until the end of the year following the donation.
- **Competência**: juiz eleitoral of the donor's domicile.
- **Polo ativo**: MP eleitoral.
- **Polo passivo**: the donor.
- **Rito**: LC 64/1990 Art. 22.
- **Prova**:
  - General rule: the author bears the burden (NCPC Art. 373 I).
  - In practice, excess donations are detected via Receita Federal
    cross-checks, so cases are typically filed after prestação de
    contas concludes.
- **Efeito**:
  - Multa of 100% of the value donated in excess.
  - 8 years inelegibilidade.
  - Both donors and recipients are sanctioned.

## Comparison of the four main electoral actions

Quick reference. Substance in §§4–7 below. For common confusions
between these (especially deadlines and effects on inelegibilidade),
see [`../pitfalls.md`](../pitfalls.md) "Electoral procedures".

| Action | Phase | Deadline | Statutory basis | Cassação? | 8y inelegibilidade? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **AIPRC** | pre-election | 5 days from publication of registration request, hard cutoff 15 Aug | LC 64/1990 Art. 3 | yes (cassação of registration) | no (refusal of registration only) |
| **AIJE** | campaign through diplomação | from registration to diplomação | LC 64/1990 Art. 22 | yes (post-Ficha Limpa) | yes (8y for those who acted + candidate) |
| **AIME** | post-election | 15 days from diplomação | CF Art. 14 §§10–11 | yes (with eficácia imediata) | only if grounded in abuso de poder econômico |
| **RCED** | post-election | 3 days from diplomação | CE Art. 262 | yes | yes (8y) |

All four actions carry **suspensive effect** of recursos against
cassação. AIME runs in **segredo de justiça**. AIJE's potencialidade
requirement was made dispensable by Ficha Limpa.

## 4. AIPRC — Ação de Impugnação ao Pedido de Registro de Candidatura

Pre-election challenge to a candidate's registration. Filed before
the candidate is on the ballot.

- **Statutory basis**: LC 64/1990 Art. 3.
- **Cabimento**: ineligibility (constitutional or LC 64), missing
  registration formalities, non-eligibility.
- **Competência**:
  - **Presidente** → TSE.
  - **Governador, Senador, Deputado** → TRE.
  - **Prefeito, Vereador** → juiz eleitoral.
- **Polo ativo**: candidate or pre-candidate; party or coligação in
  the same district; MP eleitoral (subject to the 4-year rule of no
  political activity).
- **Prazo**: 5 calendar days from publication of the registration
  request, with hard cutoff at 15 August 19:00.
- **Rito** (LC 64/1990 Art. 3): no contraditório; the candidate
  presents a justification.
- **Efeito**:
  - *Cassação* effect.
  - Recourse against cassação has *suspensive effect* — if the
    candidate appeals, they may continue campaigning (LE Art. 16-A,
    added 2009).
  - Validity of votes is conditioned on final decision; votes for a
    sub-judice candidate are *engavetados*.
  - Post-registration changes favorable to the candidate may benefit
    them up to diplomação (LE Art. 11).

## 5. AIME — Ação de Impugnação de Mandato Eletivo

Action to revoke an elected mandate obtained via corruption, abuse
of economic power, or fraud. Constitutional basis: CF Art. 14
§§10–11.

- **Cabimento**:
  - Abuso de poder econômico.
  - Fraude — including (per recent jurisprudence) fraudulent
    composition of the gender quota in proportional candidate lists
    (the LE requires at least 30% women).
  - Corrupção.
- **Polo ativo**: coligações, parties (only when challenging the
  validity of a coligação or after the election), MP eleitoral,
  candidates.
- **Polo passivo**: the candidate; the vice (RCED nº 703-SC, Súmula
  38); suplentes if candidate is for Senador; those who participated
  in the abusive conduct (REspe nº 84356/2016).
- **Competência**: TSE / TRE / juiz eleitoral per office level. **No
  foro privilegiado** — AIME is not a criminal action.
- **Prazo**: 15 calendar days after diplomação.
- **Rito**:
  - LC 64/1990 Art. 3.
  - TSE Resolução 23.554/2017.
  - Tribunal regimentos internos.
  - CE on the recursal phase.
  - **Segredo de justiça** (CF Art. 14 §11).
  - **Eficácia imediata** even when subject to recurso (overrides
    CE Art. 216).
- **Efeito**:
  - Cassação (*efeito suspensivo de recursos contra cassação*).
  - 8 years inelegibilidade — but only if the cassação is based on
    abuso de poder econômico, not corrupção or fraude.
- **Conexão**: no litispendência with other electoral actions.
- **Prova — potencialidade**: TSE jurisprudence requires that the
  conduct have *potencialidade* to influence the electoral result.
  Post-Ficha Limpa, this requirement should be read alongside the
  *gravidade das circunstâncias* criterion in LC 64/1990 Art. 22 XVI.
- **History**: created by the 1988 Constitution; never had its own
  regulating statute.

## 6. AIJE — Ação de Investigação Judicial Eleitoral

Investigation of abuse of economic power, political power, or media,
filed between candidate registration and diplomação. Statutory
basis: LC 64/1990 Art. 22.

- **Cabimento**:
  - **Abuso de poder econômico**: no direct legal definition.
    Mere irregularities don't suffice — must involve significant
    resources. Examples include violations of campaign-finance
    limits (LE Art. 17), exceeding maximum donations, "compra de
    candidatura" (REspe nº 19847/2015), mass vote-buying,
    distribution of benefits (AgReg REespe nº 60117), large sums
    from prohibited sources (LE Art. 25), caixa 2, firms providing
    structure to candidates (RO nº 4377-64/2011), employer coercion
    of workers, voter transport, and excessive propaganda.
  - **Abuso de poder político**: by political officeholders, party
    leaders, or religious leaders. Often overlaps with *condutas
    vedadas*.
  - **Abuso dos meios de comunicação social**: improper use of press,
    radio, television, books. Privileged treatment based on financial
    arrangement may also constitute economic abuse.
  - **Fraude**: electoral falsification, use of false documents.
    Until recently, fraude could only be challenged via AIME; AIJE
    now also accepted (REspe nº 24342).
- **Competência**:
  - **Presidente** → TSE (Corregedor-Geral).
  - **Governador, Senador, Deputado** → TRE (Corregedor regional).
  - **Prefeito, Vereador** → juiz eleitoral.
  - No foro privilegiado.
- **Polo ativo**: MP, (pre-)candidate, party (but not a party in a
  coligação before the election), coligação.
- **Polo passivo**: candidate; whoever helped or participated
  (LC 64/1990 Art. 22 XIV; REspe nº 84356/2016); candidate to vice
  (Súmula 38). **Not**: legal entities, coligações, or parties.
- **Prazo**: from registration until diplomação (TSE AgReg em RO nº
  10520/2015; RO 1.453/2010).
- **Rito** (LC 64/1990 Arts. 22–23):
  - Distributed to the corregedor-geral / corregedor-regional /
    juiz eleitoral.
  - Relator may suspend the act underlying the representação
    (LC 64/1990 Art. 22 Ib).
  - No formal *inquérito* — LC 64/1990 Arts. 19–22 do not authorize
    ex officio investigation, only the suspensive measure.
  - Recurso against summary indeferimento via *recurso inominado*
    (3 days; functionally an "agravo interno").
  - Vinculação da decisão ao pedido (LC 64/1990 Art. 23 + Súmula 62)
    is contested; many read it as applying only to the evidentiary
    framework.
- **Prova**:
  - LC 64/1990 Art. 23 deviates from the traditional "what is not in
    the case is not in the world" rule of evidence; constitutionality
    affirmed in STF ADI 1.082/DF.
  - **Exigência de potencialidade lesiva**: traditional requirement
    that the abuse have potential to influence the result. Made
    dispensable by Ficha Limpa (LC 64/1990 Art. 22, post-LC 135/2010).
    Still applied with substantial subjectivity.
- **Efeito**:
  - Cassação (LC 64/1990 Art. 22 XIV) — previously required AIME or
    RCED.
  - 8 years inelegibilidade from the election (LC 64/1990 Art. 22
    XIV) — applies to those who committed the acts and to the
    candidate if they contributed.
  - Suspensive effect of recursos against cassação.
  - Remessa to MP for disciplinary process and criminal action.
- **Conexão**: AIME is reserved for elected candidates; AIJE may
  group representações for *condutas vedadas*. Reunion of cases at
  the same instance is possible.

## 7. RCED — Recurso Contra a Expedição do Diploma

Post-election challenge to the diploma based on supervening or
constitutional ineligibility, or lack of eligibility. Statutory
basis: CE Art. 262.

- **Cabimento**:
  - **Inelegibilidade superveniente**: ineligibility arising between
    candidate registration and election day (Súmula 47). Examples:
    criminal conviction, improbidade administrativa.
  - **Inelegibilidade constitucional**.
  - **Falta de elegibilidade** (Lei 12.891/2013).
- **Competência**:
  - Eleições municipais → TRE (juiz eleitoral handles preparation).
  - Eleições gerais → TSE (TRE handles preparation).
  - Eleição presidencial → contested historically; STF ADPF 167
    (2018): TSE.
  - Judged by the higher instance per Súmula 37.
  - **No foro privilegiado**.
- **Polo ativo**: candidato diplomado; vice (if executive office).
- **Polo passivo**: party, coligação, candidate, MP. A coligado party
  may file isolatedly only after the election (TSE).
- **Prazo**: 3 days after diplomação (CE Art. 258). Effect of
  judicial recess on this deadline is contested.
- **Rito** (CE Art. 262): no withdrawal possible (TSE jurisprudence).
- **Prova — potencialidade**: TSE has required that the conduct have
  potencialidade to influence the electoral result when grounded in
  abuso de poder. Should be read alongside the gravidade criterion
  of LC 64/1990 Art. 22 XVI.
- **Efeito**:
  - Cassação.
  - Suspensive effect of recursos against cassação.
  - 8 years inelegibilidade.
- **Conexão**: does not lose its object due to a decision in AIJE or
  AIME. Multiple recursos against diplomação may be joined
  (NCPC Arts. 55, 57).
- **Natureza jurídica**: an action, not (only) a recurso despite its
  name.
- **History**: until 1988, the only procedure that permitted
  cassação. The 1988 Constitution introduced AIME. Lei 12.891/2013
  removed the use of RCED for abuso de poder econômico/político
  (TSE RO 881-PI/2013 had already so decided).

## 8. Ação Penal Eleitoral

Criminal prosecution of electoral crimes (CE Capítulo II — Dos Crimes
Eleitorais).

- **Statutory basis**: CE Art. 29 Id, CE Art. 35 II, CPP Art. 78.
- Substantive crimes: enumerated in the Código Eleitoral (Capítulo
  II). Includes electoral falsification, vote-buying as a criminal
  offense (parallel to the civil captação ilícita), corrupção
  eleitoral, propaganda crimes, etc.

## 9. Pedido de direito de resposta — LE Art. 58

Right-of-reply procedure for candidates targeted by calumnious,
defamatory, injurious, or knowingly false propaganda.

- **Object**: propaganda that is calumniosa, difamatória, injuriosa,
  or sabidamente inverídica.
- **Prazo**:
  - 24 hours during the horário eleitoral gratuito.
  - 48 hours for normal radio/TV programming.
  - 72 hours for written press.
  - At any time during internet circulation, with 72 hours after
    each new instance.
- **Polo ativo**: the offended party or legal representative.
- **Polo passivo**: the party responsible for the propaganda.
- **Rito**: LE Art. 58.
- **Efeito**: publication of the response in the same vehicle, space,
  page, size, and emphasis as the original.

## 10. Recursos

Recurso practice in electoral procedure has its own rules, distinct
from civil procedure. The main types:

### 10.1 Recurso Inominado Eleitoral

#### Against a juiz eleitoral (CE Art. 265)

Challenges decisions, resoluções, despachos, or final decisions in
ações or representações.

- **Object**: decisions in general — indeferimento de registro,
  filiação partidária, despachos, etc. **Not** interlocutory
  decisions.
- **Polo ativo**: candidates, parties, coligações, MP eleitoral.
- **Prazo**:
  - 3 days in general.
  - 24 hours in representações.
  - 10 days in criminal matters.
- **Competência**: TRE.
- **Rito** (CE Arts. 266–267):
  - Juiz may *retratar* the challenged decision (CE Art. 267 §7);
    if so, the recorrido may petition for the recurso to ascend.
  - Petição → razões → contrarrazões (same prazo) → MP
    manifestação.
  - As a rule, devolutive effect only (CE Art. 257).

#### Against a junta eleitoral

With the urna eletrônica, recursos against juntas eleitorais are now
rare.

- **Recurso parcial** (CE Arts. 261, 265): the line between *recurso
  parcial* and *recurso inominado* is unclear in practice.
- **Polo ativo**: fiscais eleitorais, party delegados, candidates,
  MPE.
- **Prazo**: immediately after the junta's decision; reasoned within
  48 hours.
- **Rito** (CE Art. 169): no juízo de retratação.
- **Competência**: juiz eleitoral if not part of the junta;
  otherwise TRE.

#### Against a juiz auxiliar ("juízes da propaganda")

- Recurso judged by the plenary of the TRE (LE Art. 96 §4).

### 10.2 Recurso Ordinário Eleitoral

Functions as the appeal between TRE/TSE and the next instance.
Statutory basis: CE Art. 276 II a–b; CF Art. 121 §§3–4.

- **Cabimento**:
  - **From a TRE decision** denying HC, mandado de segurança, habeas
    data, or mandado de injunção; or on inelegibilidades or diplomas
    in federal/state elections. It suffices that the underlying
    action *could in theory* produce inelegibilidade (REspe nº
    282675/2012).
  - **From a TSE decision** denying HC, mandado de segurança
    (debated whether habeas data and mandado de injunção also
    apply).
- **Polo ativo**: candidate, party, coligação, MPE.
- **Prazo**: 3 days from publication of the acórdão in the Diário
  Oficial; or 3 days from diplomação if the recurso is about the
  diploma.
- **Efeito**: devolutive (CE Art. 257).
- **Rito**: no juízo de admissibilidade.
- **Competência**: from TRE → TSE; from TSE → STF.

### 10.3 Recurso Especial Eleitoral

The TSE-level analog of the federal recurso especial. Statutory
basis: CF Art. 121 §4 I–II; CE Art. 276 I a–b.

- Reserved for contradiction with the CF or for resolving disparity
  between TREs.
- Does **not** admit reexame de fatos e provas.
- Requires *exhaustion of instance*.

#### Requisitos

1. **Decisão terminativa de TRE** (CF Art. 121 §4).
2. **Impossibility of fact/evidence reexame**: questions about the
   admission and legal valuation of evidence are admissible; if the
   acórdão judges evidence "insufficient", that adjective itself is
   reviewable. The line is blurry in practice.
3. **Prequestionamento**: the TSE cannot be the first body to
   examine controversial legal questions. Constitutional questions
   likewise cannot be raised first at the TSE.
4. **Decisão contra a CF**: contradiction with the constitution
   (does not need to be direct, but must be substantive). Generic
   allegations are insufficient. Not admissible if TSE jurisprudence
   already aligns with the recurrida decision.
5. **Divergência entre TREs ou em relação ao TSE**: admissible if
   the recorrida contradicts another TRE. Comparison must be
   substantive: identify the facts behind both acórdãos and how the
   legal rules were differently applied. Not admissible if the
   recorrida aligns with TSE jurisprudence (Súmula 30) or applies
   consolidated STF precedent.
6. **No juízo de admissibilidade** if about registro de candidatura
   (Ag nº 2447/2000).

#### Tramitação

- Initial review by the President of the TRE before forwarding to
  the TSE.

### 10.4 Recurso Extraordinário (CF Art. 102, 121 §3)

- Admissible only against decisions of the TSE (TSE AgR-AI 9.569/2010).
- Constitutional questions only.

### 10.5 Embargos de Declaração (CE Art. 275)

Standard purposes: obscuridade, contradição, omissão.

- **Polo ativo**: parties to the proceeding from the start —
  candidate, MPE, party, coligação.
- **Prazo**: 3 days from publication of the acórdão or sentença;
  24 hours in representações.
- **Efeito**: not suspensive; **interruptive** of other recursal
  prazos; not infringente — the judge may only complement, not
  alter, the original decision.
- **Competência**: same as the original decision.
- **Natureza jurídica**: recurso (CE Art. 275, post-2015).
- **Rito**: petition to the relator (or juiz eleitoral); placed for
  judgment in the next session; juiz eleitoral has 3 days by analogy
  with CE Art. 258.
- **Embargos intempestivos / protelatórios**: do not interrupt the
  prazo; subject to multa (NCPC Art. 1026 §§2–3).
- **History**: there was no statutory provision for embargos against
  decisions of juízes eleitorais until the NCPC (2015).

### 10.6 Agravo Interno

Formerly called *agravo regimental*. Statutory basis: TSE Regimento
Interno Art. 36 §8; TRE regimentos; NCPC Art. 1021.

- **Object**: reformation of a relator's monocratic decision by
  taking it to the plenário.
- **Polo ativo**: the recurrente disconforme with the relator's
  decision.
- **Prazo**: 3 days (24 hours in representações).
- **Rito**:
  - Cannot simply repeat the prior recurso's reasoning.
  - Filed with the relator.
  - If the relator maintains the decision, it goes to the Pleno;
    the original decision counts as the first vote.
  - Placed in mesa for judgment; no need for inclusion in pauta.
  - No oral argument.
  - Processed in the same autos.

### 10.7 Agravo de Instrumento

Available only against:

- Inadmission of recurso especial by the President of the TRE
  (CE Art. 279).
- Inadmission of recurso extraordinário by the President of the TSE
  (CE Art. 282).

- **Prazo**: 3 days.
- **Efeito**: permanent; devolutive (exception per NCPC Art. 995).
- **Competência**: TRE or TSE.
- **Rito**: processed in the same autos (since Lei 12.322/2010).

## 11. Ação Rescisória Eleitoral

Statutory basis: CE Art. 22 j (added 1996; not previously admitted).

- **Prazo**: 120 days.
- **Cabimento**: NCPC Art. 485 IX (transposed from old CPC).
- **Note on mandate exercise**: the politician retains the mandate
  until trânsito em julgado of the rescisória (STF ADI 1459/1999
  declared the contrary unconstitutional).
- **Restrictions**:
  - TREs cannot judge rescisórias against their own decisions
    (TSE AR 89/2001).
  - Only AIJE, AIPRC, and RCED decisions may be rescinded.
