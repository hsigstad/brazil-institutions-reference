# Ministério Público

The Brazilian MP is a permanent institution, independent of the three
branches, charged with defending the legal order, the democratic regime,
and *interesses sociais e individuais indisponíveis* (CF/88 Art. 127).
It is neither "prosecutor" nor "attorney general" in the US sense — it
is a functionally independent fourth institution with constitutional
autonomy.

This file covers the structure, career, powers, and internal organs of
the MP. For the specific role of the MP in civil corruption prosecution,
see `improbidade.md`. For criminal corruption proceedings, see
`anticorrupcao-penal.md`. For the constitutional guarantees common to
judges and MP members, see `funcoes-essenciais.md` (historical notes)
and `juizes.md`.

**Topics / keywords**: Ministério Público, MP, MPF, MPE, MPU, MPT, MPM,
MPDFT, MP de Contas, MPC, promotor de justiça, procurador da República,
procurador-geral, PGR, CNMP, inquérito civil, IC, TAC, termo de
ajustamento de conduta, ação civil pública, ACP, GAECO, CAOs, promotor
natural, Ficha Limpa MP, vitaliciedade, LOMP, LONMP.

**Snapshot as of 2026**: constitutional structure is stable
(CF 127–130). Lei Complementar 75/1993 (LC MPU) governs federal MP;
Lei 8.625/1993 (LONMP) governs state MPs. Lei 14.230/2021 made the MP
the exclusive holder of standing in improbidade actions (see
`improbidade.md`). CNMP composition and rules updated periodically by
constitutional amendment (EC 45/2004 created it).

Compiled from personal research notes on the Ministério Público,
complementing the constitutional overview in `funcoes-essenciais.md`.

## 1. Constitutional framework (CF Arts. 127–130)

### Mission (Art. 127)

"Instituição permanente, essencial à função jurisdicional do Estado,
incumbindo-lhe a defesa da ordem jurídica, do regime democrático e dos
interesses sociais e individuais indisponíveis."

Key principles (Art. 127 §1):

- **Unidade** — one institution speaking with one voice.
- **Indivisibilidade** — members can substitute for each other without
  affecting the institution.
- **Independência funcional** — each member acts according to conviction
  in the case before them; superiors cannot dictate legal positions.

### Autonomy (Art. 127 §§2–6)

- **Autonomia funcional e administrativa**.
- Own budget proposal subject to LDO limits.
- Independent financial management.

### Functions (Art. 129)

- **Criminal prosecution** (I): exclusive dominus litis of the public
  criminal action.
- **Civil defense of the public patrimony and social interests** (III):
  via ação civil pública (Lei 7.347/1985) and ação de improbidade
  administrativa (Lei 8.429/1992).
- **Defense of indigenous, minority, consumer, environment, cultural
  heritage rights** (III, V).
- **Inquérito civil**: exclusive investigative instrument for non-criminal
  matters (III).
- **Control of external police activity** (VII).
- **Request of investigative measures and institution of inquérito
  policial**, with requisição powers (VIII).

### Guarantees (Art. 128 §5 I)

Members of the MP enjoy:

- **Vitaliciedade** after 2 years (only judicial action can remove).
- **Inamovibilidade** (cannot be transferred without consent, except by
  public-interest decision of the órgão colegiado competente).
- **Irredutibilidade de subsídio**.

### Vedações (Art. 128 §5 II)

Members are forbidden from:

- Receiving honorários or custas processuais.
- Exercising advocacia (private practice).
- Participating in sociedade comercial.
- Exercising other public function (except teaching).
- Political activity.
- Receiving auxílios from private persons.

## 2. Organizational structure

### Ministério Público da União (MPU)

Governed by **LC 75/1993** (Lei Orgânica do MPU, "LOMPU"). Comprises
four specialized branches:

- **MPF — Ministério Público Federal**: federal criminal and civil cases,
  federal interests, federal funds, indigenous affairs. Members are
  **Procuradores da República** (first instance) → **Procuradores
  Regionais da República** (TRF level) → **Subprocuradores-Gerais da
  República** (superior courts).
- **MPT — Ministério Público do Trabalho**: labor-law public interests;
  acts before the Justiça do Trabalho. Procuradores do Trabalho.
- **MPM — Ministério Público Militar**: acts before the Justiça Militar
  da União.
- **MPDFT — Ministério Público do Distrito Federal e Territórios**:
  functions like a state MP for DF.

**Chief**: **Procurador-Geral da República (PGR)**, appointed by the
President from career members over 35, after Senate majority approval;
2-year term, one reappointment allowed (CF Art. 128 §1). By tradition
(post-Lula), the PGR is chosen from a *lista tríplice* elaborated by the
ANPR (Associação Nacional dos Procuradores da República), though the
tripla list has no legal force.

The PGR is also the chief of the MPF specifically and heads the MPU as
a whole.

### Ministério Público Estadual (MPE)

Governed by **Lei 8.625/1993** (Lei Orgânica Nacional do MP, "LONMP")
and each state's own Lei Orgânica.

- Each state has its own MPE with its own budget, career, and internal
  structure.
- **Chief**: **Procurador-Geral de Justiça (PGJ)**, appointed by the
  governor from a *lista tríplice* elaborated by the MPE career members
  (CF Art. 128 §3); 2-year term with one reappointment. In RJ, state law
  requires choosing from the list; in other states the governor has more
  discretion but typically respects the list.
- **Destituição**: by majority of the state legislature (CF Art. 128 §4).

### Career structure (state MPs)

- **Promotor de Justiça**: first-instance member. Typical career entry
  via concurso público.
- **Procurador de Justiça**: second-instance member, acts before TJ.
- **Entrâncias** (tiers): **Inicial → Intermediária → Final**. Small
  comarcas = inicial; state capital = final. Career advancement via
  *promoção* (antiguidade or merecimento) between entrâncias. See
  `carreira-juizes.md` for the parallel judicial career structure.

### Career movements (MP-SP)

- **Promoção**: permanent move between entrâncias (antiguidade or
  merecimento).
- **Remoção**: permanent transfer within the same entrância.
- **Permuta**: mutual swap between two prosecutors.
- **Designação**: temporary assignment (vacation, leave coverage).
- In MP-SP, on the order of ~150 permanent movements per year occur
  across the ~319 comarcas (rough scale from internal observation).

## 3. Ministério Público de Contas (MPC)

A distinct branch operating **before the Tribunais de Contas** (TCU, TCEs,
TCMs), not before the judiciary. Often overlooked because it is separate
from the MPU/MPE career tracks.

- **Function**: represents the interest of the legal order within audit
  court proceedings. Issues pareceres on accounts, files
  representações, appeals TCE decisions internally.
- **Career**: separate concurso; members are **Procuradores de Contas**.
- **Constitutional basis**: CF Art. 130 extends MP guarantees and
  vedações "no que couber" to MPC members.
- **Practical role**: in contas de prefeito cases, the MPC opinion
  precedes the conselheiro's vote on the parecer prévio (see
  `contas-municipais.md` §6).

## 4. Internal organs and specialization

### CAOs — Centros de Apoio Operacional

Thematic support units within each MPE. Organized by subject matter
(patrimônio público, meio ambiente, consumidor, infância e juventude,
criminal, etc.). CAOs do **not** hold case files themselves; they
provide technical support, legal memos, institutional coordination, and
training to the promotores who hold cases in the field.

### GAECO — Grupo de Atuação Especial de Combate ao Crime Organizado

- Specialized unit for organized crime and complex criminal prosecution.
- Exists at the MPE level (each state) and at the MPF level.
- Coordinates interceptações, *colaborações premiadas*, joint operations
  with Polícia Civil / Polícia Federal.
- Central actor in most high-profile corruption operations (Lava Jato
  was led largely out of MPF force-tasks, not the GAECO brand, but the
  operational model is similar).

### Patrimônio Público specialization

- Capital-city MPE typically has **Promotores de Justiça do Patrimônio
  Público**, specialized in improbidade, licitação fraud, and
  administrative irregularities.
- In São Paulo, dedicated Promotores de Patrimônio Público handle
  improbidade at the capital. In small comarcas, the single assigned
  promotor handles improbidade alongside all other case types.

### Other specialized positions

- **Promotor de Justiça Criminal** — general criminal.
- **Promotor de Justiça da Infância e Juventude** — child welfare, ECA.
- **Promotor de Justiça do Consumidor** — consumer-protection CDC.
- **Promotor de Justiça da Cidadania / Direitos Humanos** — constitutional
  and human-rights matters.

## 5. Investigative instruments

### Inquérito Civil (IC)

- Exclusive to the MP; not a judicial proceeding, not available to
  other actors.
- Investigative phase for non-criminal matters: civil improbidade,
  environmental harm, consumer violations, public-interest violations.
- **Procedural rules**: Resolução CNMP 23/2007 (updated by subsequent
  resoluções).
- **Timeline**: should conclude within 1 year, typically extended.
- Can be shelved (*arquivamento*) with internal review by the *Conselho
  Superior do MP* of the respective body.

### Notícia de Fato (NF)

- Initial intake of a report of irregularity. Must be processed within
  **120 days** (extendable once). Ends with either dismissal or
  escalation to IC / direct action.

### TAC — Termo de Ajustamento de Conduta

- **Legal basis**: Lei 7.347/1985 Art. 5 §6 (Lei da ACP), plus sectoral
  statutes.
- Negotiated agreement between the MP and a violator, in which the
  violator commits to correct conduct, pay compensation, or perform
  remedial acts. Has **eficácia de título executivo extrajudicial**.
- Extensively used in environmental, consumer, and public-administration
  cases as an alternative to litigation.

### Acordo de não persecução cível (improbidade)

Post-Lei 14.230/2021 Art. 17-B: formal settlement between MP and
improbidade defendant, subject to judicial homologation. See
`improbidade.md` §2.

### Acordo de não persecução penal (criminal)

- **Legal basis**: CPP Art. 28-A, added by the *Pacote Anticrime*
  (Lei 13.964/2019).
- Alternative to criminal prosecution for offenses without violence,
  maximum penalty < 4 years, first-time offender conditions. Subject to
  judicial homologation.

### Colaboração premiada

- Formal cooperation agreements in organized-crime cases under Lei
  12.850/2013 Art. 4. See `anticorrupcao-penal.md` for details.

### Requisições

- The MP has constitutional requisição powers (Art. 129 VI, VIII) to
  demand documents, information, and investigative measures from public
  authorities, including instituição de inquérito policial.

## 6. Civil actions led by the MP

### Ação Civil Pública (ACP) — Lei 7.347/1985

- Instrument for collective-interest litigation: environment, consumer,
  public patrimony, historical/cultural heritage.
- MP has primary (but not exclusive) standing; also available to the
  Defensoria Pública, public entities, and qualifying associations.
- **Legitimidade concorrente**: other entities can file ACPs, but the MP
  files the majority and must be heard as fiscal da lei in ACPs filed
  by others.

### Ação de Improbidade Administrativa — Lei 8.429/1992

- Post-Lei 14.230/2021, the MP has **exclusive standing** (Art. 17
  caput). See `improbidade.md` for full treatment.

### Other instruments

- **Ação popular** (Lei 4.717/1965): any citizen may file; MP intervenes
  as fiscal da lei.
- **Ação declaratória, mandado de segurança coletivo, ação rescisória**
  in relevant cases.

## 7. CNMP — Conselho Nacional do Ministério Público

Created by **EC 45/2004** (Reforma do Judiciário), analogous to the CNJ
for the judiciary.

- **Composition**: 14 members (CF Art. 130-A):
  - PGR (presides).
  - 4 MPU members.
  - 3 MPE members.
  - 2 judges (1 STF indication, 1 STJ indication).
  - 2 lawyers (OAB indications).
  - 2 citizens (1 Câmara, 1 Senado).
- **Term**: 2 years, one reappointment.
- **Functions**: administrative and disciplinary oversight of all MP
  branches; issues resoluções that bind MP conduct; processes
  reclamações disciplinares.
- **Key resoluções**: Res. 23/2007 (inquérito civil); Res. 174/2017
  (notícia de fato); Res. 181/2017 (investigação criminal pelo MP);
  Res. 118/2014 (autocomposição).

### External control function

- The CNMP cannot override substantive legal positions (independência
  funcional is preserved) but can sanction administrative or ethical
  violations, including for promotores who act with manifest disregard
  of the law.

## 8. Selection and discretion

The MP exercises broad prosecutorial discretion. For research, this
creates major selection concerns:

- **Which complaints become inquéritos**: depends on promotor priorities,
  available resources, political context, and case salience.
- **Which inquéritos become ACPs / ACPs de improbidade**: further
  selection at the promotor level.
- **Which become TAC agreements** vs. litigated cases: varies with
  promotor style and case complexity.
- **Selection is structurally non-random** and varies across comarcas,
  entrâncias, and specialized vs. generalist promotors.

No public MP-SP report systematically documents investigation-to-prosecution
rates. Research that uses the MP caseload as an outcome must address this
selection explicitly.
