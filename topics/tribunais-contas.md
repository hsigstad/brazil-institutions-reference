# Tribunais de Contas

External fiscal control bodies (audit courts) that review public accounts.
This file covers organization, relator assignment mechanics, and oversight
procedures. For the substantive judgment of mayoral accounts and its
interaction with Ficha Limpa, see `contas-municipais.md`.

**Topics / keywords**: audit courts, fiscal control, TCU, TCE, TCM,
external control, parecer prévio, conselheiro, relator assignment,
Audesp, procurement oversight, random assignment, judicial independence.

**Snapshot as of 2026**: TCE-SP Regimento Interno references are current
through Resolução 16/2024. Conselheiro composition rules are stable
(constitutional).

Compiled from personal research notes on TCE-SP procedure and Audesp.

---

## Constitutional basis

- **CF/88 Arts. 70–75** — external fiscal control (contábil, financeira,
  orçamentária, operacional, patrimonial).
- **Art. 70**: Congress exercises fiscal oversight with the assistance of
  the TCU.
- **Art. 71**: lists TCU competences, including parecer prévio on
  presidential accounts (I) and judging accounts of administrators of public
  funds (II). The Art. 71 I/II split maps onto the contas de governo /
  contas de gestão distinction.
- **Art. 75**: extends the same rules, "no que couber", to Tribunais de
  Contas dos Estados (TCEs), Distrito Federal, and existing Tribunais /
  Conselhos de Contas dos Municípios (TCMs).
- **CF Art. 31 §4**: prohibits creating new municipal-level Tribunais de
  Contas (grandfathers existing TCMs).

**Source:** [CF/1988 Arts. 70–75](https://www.planalto.gov.br/ccivil_03/constituicao/constituicao.htm).

## Organization

- **TCU**: federal audit court. Framework statute: Lei 8.443/1992.
- **TCEs**: one per state (26 + DF). As of 2026, only **Bahia, Goiás, and
  Pará** maintain active state-level TCMs. **Rio de Janeiro** and
  **São Paulo** have municipal TCMs for their capital cities specifically.
  (CF Art. 31 §4 prohibits new TCMs; this list only shrinks.)
- **Composition**: typically 7 conselheiros. Appointment: 4 by the state
  assembly, 3 by the governor (1 auditor, 1 MP de Contas member, 1 free
  choice) — mirroring TCU rules per CF Art. 75.
- Conselheiros are political appointees, raising questions about TCE
  independence.

## TCE-SP — Tribunal de Contas do Estado de São Paulo

### Jurisdiction

- All 644 non-capital municipalities in São Paulo state (count as of
  ~2024; IBGE municipal counts can change via state-level emancipations).
- São Paulo capital has its own **TCM-SP**.
- Reviews annual accounts, audits specific acts (including procurement),
  issues opinions, imposes sanctions.

### Audesp system

Automated data collection mandated by TCE-SP. Municipalities must upload
detailed procurement and contract data to **Audesp** (Auditoria Eletrônica
de Órgãos Públicos):

- Licitações (bidding processes)
- Ajustes (contracts)
- Bid-level information, firm identifiers (CNPJ)
- Contract values and amendments

Audesp is the primary source of structured municipal procurement data
for research on São Paulo state.

### Oversight mechanisms

- **Annual account reviews**: TCE-SP issues parecer prévio on municipal
  contas de governo; municipal câmara votes on approval.
- **Specific audits**: TCE-SP can audit individual procurement processes,
  especially those flagged by complaints or automated screening.
- **Sanctions**: multas, impugnação de atos, inabilitação of officials.
- **Relationship to improbidade**: TCE findings can trigger MP
  investigations but are separate proceedings. A TCE finding of irregularity
  does **not** constitute a judicial finding of improbidade.

### Relator assignment (TCE-SP Regimento Interno)

Each processo is assigned a relator (rapporteur conselheiro) who conducts
the review and drafts the opinion. Assignment follows the Regimento Interno
(compilado, com Resolução 16/2024):

- **Random electronic draw** (Art. 36): all processos distributed "mediante
  registro em sistema eletrônico, sempre por sorteio" — by electronic
  random draw. Distribution is equitable across conselheiros and
  conselheiros substitutos-auditores (Art. 35).
- **Annual accounts (contas)** have specific rules (Art. 38):
  - Governor's accounts: rotation by seniority (I).
  - Mayor/câmara accounts: random draw, **excluding the conselheiro who
    served as relator in either of the two preceding fiscal years** (II).
  - Conselheiro substituto-auditor accounts: same exclusion rule (III).
- **Prevenção** (Art. 40, IV): once assigned, the relator becomes the
  "julgador certo" for all subsequent acts in the same processo — appeals,
  modifications, related proceedings. Creates persistence within a case
  but does not affect the initial random assignment.
- **Conselheiros substitutos-auditores** participate in the same
  distribution pool on equal footing with full conselheiros (Art. 35).

**Empirical implication:** The random draw + two-year exclusion produces
high year-over-year relator turnover for contas processos, which can be
exploited as a quasi-experimental source of variation in relator identity
for causal research on audit outcomes.

## Cross-state variation

- Terminology, procedures, and rigor vary across TCEs.
- Some TCEs are more active/strict than others — cross-state heterogeneity
  in rejection rates partly reflects institutional differences, not just
  variation in fiscal management.
