# Polícia Federal

Federal judicial police and one of the five órgãos de segurança
pública of `CF.144`. Subordinated to the Executivo via Ministério da
Justiça e Segurança Pública, but with expanded operational autonomy
since the early 2000s. Principal investigative actor in federal
corruption, narcotics, money laundering, and crimes of interstate or
international repercussion. Works in a triangular relationship with
the MPF (which requisitions and receives inquéritos) and the Justiça
Federal (which authorizes intrusive measures and tries the resulting
cases).

For MP side, see `ministerio-publico.md`. For federal judiciary, see
`justica-federal.md`. For state-level police (Polícia Civil, Polícia
Militar, Guarda Municipal), see `policias-estaduais.md`. For specific
criminal statutes PF investigates, see `anticorrupcao-penal.md`. For
the broader web of accountability institutions this PF sits in, see
`fluxo-corrupcao-municipal.md` and `fluxo-transferencia-federal.md`.

**Topics / keywords**: Polícia Federal, PF, DPF, polícia judiciária da
União, delegado de polícia federal, superintendência, operações,
inquérito policial, requisição, Lei 4.483/1964, Lei 10.446/2002,
triangular autonomy, Lava Jato, ABIN, DRCI, COAF, Interpol,
federalization of investigation, internal affairs, corregedoria.

**Snapshot as of 2026**: post-Lava Jato institutional settling.
Operational autonomy reinforced after 2021 STF Lula/Moro rulings and
the 2020 Moro resignation confrontation over DG appointment. MP
autonomous investigative power settled by STF `RE593727` (Tema 184,
2015), operationalized via CNMP Resolução 181/2017 — establishing a
partial MP substitute for PF investigation in the criminal track.

## 1. Constitutional and legal framework

### Constitutional basis (`CF.144.§1`)

PF is a permanent federal body (`CF.144.§1`) with four constitutional
functions:

- Investigate crimes against federal political/social order or
  against União interests, and crimes of interstate/international
  repercussion that require uniform repression "segundo se dispuser
  em lei" (`CF.144.§1.I`).
- Prevent and repress illicit narcotraffic, contraband, and
  descaminho, without prejudice to fazendária and hacendística
  action (CF Art. 144 §1 II).
- Exercise polícia marítima, aeroportuária, and de fronteiras
  (`CF.144.§1.III`).
- **Exclusively** exercise the functions of polícia judiciária da
  União (`CF.144.§1.IV`) — the exclusivity clause is load-bearing for
  PF's jurisdictional reach over federal criminal investigation.

### Legal basis — jurisdiction and organization

Historical: Lei 4.483/1964 gave the then-DFSP national jurisdiction.
Renamed Departamento de Polícia Federal (DPF) in 1967.

Current: Lei 10.446/2002 implements `CF.144.§1.I` by enumerating
specific crime categories where PF has investigative authority
regardless of whether interstate repercussion is documented — human
trafficking, firearm-smuggling, environmental crimes against
indigenous/federal assets, financial-system crimes, and others. This
statute is the operational answer to the "repercussão interestadual"
gatekeeper of `CF.144.§1.I`.

Organic regulations: Lei 9.266/1996 (carreira policial federal),
regulamented by Decretos and successive reorganizations. Current
estrutura by Decreto 9.662/2019 and amendments.

### MP–PF interaction (`CF.129.VII`, `CF.129.VIII`)

- MP exercises **controle externo da atividade policial**
  (`CF.129.VII`), through CNMP Resolução 20/2007 (state MPs) and
  equivalent MPF resolutions.
- MP has constitutional power to **requisitar diligências e
  instauração de inquérito policial** (`CF.129.VIII`).
- PF retains **operational autonomy**: MP cannot direct the daily
  conduct of an inquérito; it can requisition specific measures and
  the opening of an inquiry, but not command the investigation
  moment-to-moment.
- **Post-Tema 184**: STF `RE593727` (2015) recognized MP's autonomous
  power to conduct criminal investigations (PIC — Procedimento
  Investigatório Criminal, CNMP Resolução 181/2017). This creates a
  partial substitute for PF investigation that the MP can deploy
  directly, especially in cases where PF is slow or conflicted.

## 2. Organization

### Structure

- **Direção-Geral (DG)**: presides over PF; appointed by the
  President, traditionally a career delegado. 2020 crisis: Moro
  resignation over Bolsonaro attempt to replace DG — established
  (informally) that the DG should be a career officer insulated from
  direct political direction.
- **Superintendências Regionais**: one per state, plus subsidiary
  Delegacias Descentralizadas in interior cities. Geographic
  distribution is uneven — small states may have one or two offices
  total.
- **Unidades especializadas** (Brasília HQ):
  - DICOR — Diretoria de Investigação e Combate ao Crime Organizado.
  - DCOR / DRCI — financial and money-laundering investigations;
    Brazilian interlocutor for FATF/GAFI coordination and
    international cooperation.
  - Interpol Brasil — Brazilian Interpol NCB, located within PF.
  - Corregedoria-Geral — internal affairs, PAD for federal police
    members.

### Career

- **Delegado de Polícia Federal** — heads investigations, presides
  over inquéritos, constitutional counterpart of the delegado de
  polícia in the states. Law degree required; concurso público.
- **Agente, Escrivão, Papiloscopista, Perito Criminal Federal** —
  operational careers requiring higher education but not law degree.
- All careers under Lei 9.266/1996 and subsequent regulations.

## 3. Investigative instruments

### Inquérito policial

- Primary PF investigative instrument (`CPP.4`–`CPP.23`). Formal
  administrative proceeding headed by a delegado federal.
- Opened by MP requisição (`CF.129.VIII`), judicial requisição,
  citizen complaint, or PF own initiative.
- Concluded with a **relatório** sent to MPF (through the judge,
  procedurally) who decides on denúncia, arquivamento, or further
  investigation.
- PF cannot prosecute directly; denúncia is MPF's exclusive function
  (CF Art. 129 I).

### Operações especiais

- Named operations are PF's characteristic public modality: coordinated
  simultaneous actions (busca e apreensão, prisões temporárias,
  quebras de sigilo) executed with MPF and judicial authorization.
- Functionally a combination of **investigative instrument** (gathers
  evidence) and **accountability instrument** (publicizes wrongdoing,
  generates pressure for institutional response). Media publicity is
  integral to the model, not incidental.
- Since the early 2000s, operações have become the dominant public
  face of PF action. The term "operação" has no distinct legal
  status — procedurally, these are inquéritos policiais executed in
  coordinated fashion.

### Intrusive measures — judicial reserve

All intrusive measures (interceptação telefônica, quebra de sigilo
bancário/fiscal, busca domiciliar, prisão temporária, prisão
preventiva) require **prior judicial authorization**:

- Interceptação: Lei 9.296/1996 (Lei 9.296/1996); 15 days renewable.
- Sigilo bancário: LC 105/2001; judicial order required (except for
  direct MP/CGU/TCU access in specific administrative contexts).
- Busca e apreensão: `CPP.240`–`CPP.250`.
- Prisão temporária: Lei 7.960/1989, 5 days renewable once.

## 4. Relationship with other accountability institutions

### PF–MPF (triangular doctrine)

Operational division of labor:

- PF conducts the investigation (inquérito policial).
- MPF oversees, requisitions measures, and ultimately prosecutes
  (denúncia).
- Judiciary authorizes intrusive measures and tries the case.

Friction points:

- MP investigative autonomy (PIC, post-Tema 184) lets MP bypass PF
  when necessary, reducing PF's monopoly on criminal investigation.
- MP can refuse to file denúncia on a PF-produced inquérito
  (arquivamento) — subject to judicial review (`CPP.28`) and, for
  MPF, internal review by the CCR-2 (2ª Câmara de Coordenação e
  Revisão).

### PF–CGU

Division of labor on federal fund misuse (see `cgu-controle-interno.md`):

- CGU: controle interno, administrative audit, PAD for federal
  servants.
- PF: criminal investigation of the same facts.
- Typical flow: CGU audit identifies irregularity → referral to MPF
  → MPF requisitions PF inquérito if criminal facts are present.
- Joint force-task models (Lava Jato template) combine PF, MPF, CGU,
  RFB, and COAF personnel in integrated operations.

### PF–COAF

- COAF (Conselho de Controle de Atividades Financeiras) produces RIFs
  (Relatórios de Inteligência Financeira) that feed PF and MPF
  investigations (see `anticorrupcao-penal.md` §2).
- RIFs are **intelligence**, not evidence — they direct investigation
  but must be re-produced through regular procedural channels (e.g.,
  judicial quebra de sigilo) to ground a conviction.

## 5. Accountability concerns

### Politicization

Because PF is subordinate to the Executivo, each federal
administration retains significant influence over PF direction
through DG appointment, budget, and internal priorities. Recurrent
concerns:

- Selection of which operações to launch (timing, targets) as a
  politically consequential choice.
- Executive pressure on ongoing investigations that implicate the
  governing coalition.
- 2020 Moro–Bolsonaro confrontation — framed as a constitutional
  crisis over PF autonomy.

### Internal corruption

PF itself is not immune from corruption. Documented historical
episodes include PF delegados and agentes arrested in PF-led
operations (Arantes 2011 documents 60 PF officers arrested in
cross-operational data 2003--2008). The corregedoria-geral of the PF
handles internal affairs but has limited external visibility.

## 6. Research design implications

### Observation and selection

- **Operações as data source**: PF publishes named operations in its
  communications portal (Agência de Notícias PF). Coded datasets of
  operations by crime type, location, and institutional target exist
  in secondary sources (Arantes 2011 covers 2003--2008; subsequent
  work has extended coverage).
- **Selection into operação is non-random**: conditional on a case
  entering PF, the decision to organize a named operação (vs. routine
  inquiry) is discretionary and reflects MPF strategy, PF priority,
  and political consequence.
- **Geographic coverage is uneven**: correlation between state GDP
  and operação count is high (Arantes 2011 reports 88% correlation
  for 2003--2008). Small/poor states are underpoliced by PF, which
  has research-design implications for any cross-state comparison of
  detected corruption.

### Timing

- Inquéritos policiais typically run 30--90 days per phase but can
  extend for years in complex cases with renewal.
- Operação timing is endogenous — PF and MPF time actions based on
  electoral cycles, media windows, and judicial availability.

### Jurisdictional ambiguity

- `CF.144.§1.I`'s "repercussão interestadual" requirement is
  interpreted broadly; in practice, much PF activity covers facts
  that could also be investigated by state civil police. Lei
  10.446/2002 enumerates specific crime classes where PF has
  unambiguous authority.

## 8. Key jurisprudence

- **STF `RE593727`** (Tema 184, decided 2015): MP has autonomous power
  to conduct criminal investigations — confirmed against long-running
  challenge from the delegados' lobby. Operationalized by CNMP
  Resolução 181/2017.
- **STF `LulaMoro2021`**: recognized former judge Sergio Moro's
  parcialidade in Lava Jato cases against Lula; annulled convictions.
  Reshaped PF–MPF–judiciary triangle by making clear that even when
  PF operations are technically regular, the downstream judicial
  process must remain independent.
- **STF `ADI6298`** (Aug 2023): juiz das garantias (`CPP.3-A`, `LPC`)
  upheld. Formalizes the investigation/trial judge separation, with
  implications for PF investigation oversight.
