# Contas municipais — fiscal oversight of mayors

Constitutional, statutory, and jurisprudential framework for the annual
judgment of mayoral accounts: the TCE parecer prévio, the câmara vote,
the 2/3 override rule, and the Ficha Limpa ineligibility consequences.
For TCE organization, relator assignment, and the contas de governo /
contas de gestão distinction at the institutional level, see
`tribunais-contas.md`. For the LRF as a fiscal-federalism instrument
and broader transfer mechanics, see `federalismo-fiscal.md`. For
campaign finance accounts, see `prestacao-contas-eleitorais.md`. For
the improbidade consequence of a rejected account, see `improbidade.md`.

**Topics / keywords**: mayoral accounts, fiscal oversight, câmara
municipal, parecer prévio, contas de governo, contas de gestão, Ficha
Limpa, ineligibility, 2/3 override, secret vs. open voting, Lei de
Responsabilidade Fiscal, LRF, STF Tema 157, ADPF 982, EC 76/2013.

**Snapshot as of 2026**: STF jurisprudence on contas de governo /
gestão is current through `ADPF982` (2025). The set of municipalities
that have reformed to open voting is a living list — the examples
below are illustrative, not a systematic census.

---

## 1. Constitutional anchor — `CF.31`

The load-bearing rule is `CF.31.§2`: the TCE parecer prévio on the
mayor's annual accounts "somente deixará de prevalecer por decisão de
dois terços dos membros da Câmara Municipal." The TCE recommendation
prevails by default; only a 2/3 câmara override can reverse it. This
single clause is the institutional architecture from which the rest
of this file follows.

Other relevant clauses:

- `CF.31.§3`: municipal accounts are open to taxpayer inspection for
  60 days per year.
- `CF.31.§4`: creation of new municipal-level Tribunais de Contas is
  prohibited (existing TCMs grandfathered — São Paulo and Rio de
  Janeiro municipalities and a handful of others).
- `CF.31.§1`: external control is exercised by the câmara with TCE/TCM
  assistance.

The contas de governo / contas de gestão distinction is rooted in
`CF.71.I`/`CF.71.II` (parecer prévio vs. judgment of administrators);
that institutional split is described in `tribunais-contas.md`.

**No federal rule** on secret vs. open voting in câmaras municipais —
each municipality regulates the act of judging contas through its Lei
Orgânica and Regimento Interno (`CF.29`). Load-bearing for the
diffusion analysis in §4.

## 2. Contas de governo vs. contas de gestão

Determines (a) who has final say over the accounts and (b) whether
rejection triggers Ficha Limpa ineligibility.

- **Contas de governo**: the mayor's annual consolidated accounts —
  budgetary execution, fiscal targets, compliance with fiscal rules.
  TCE issues parecer prévio; câmara has exclusive authority to
  approve or reject (`CF.31.§2`, `CF.71.I`).
- **Contas de gestão**: the mayor's acts as *ordenador de despesas* —
  specific contracts, payments, procurement. TCE judges these
  directly and can impose sanctions (`CF.71.II`).

Contested for years; resolved in two STF decisions:

### `Tema157` — RE 848.826 (decided 2016-08-10, certified 2019-10-18)

Vote 6–5. **Both** contas de governo and contas de gestão fall under
câmara jurisdiction *for Ficha Limpa ineligibility purposes*. TCE
parecer alone does not generate ineligibility — câmara must vote to
reject (ordinary majority to follow TCE, 2/3 to override). Omission
does not generate ineligibility: "inadmissível o julgamento ficto das
contas por decurso de prazo." See `../jurisprudencia-stf.md` for
canonical description and dissent line-up.

### `ADPF982` (decided 2025)

Reaffirmed that TCEs can independently judge contas de gestão and
impose administrative sanctions (débitos, multas) on mayors as
ordenadores de despesas. **Electoral ineligibility** under Ficha
Limpa arises only from câmara rejection of contas de governo, not
from TCE decisions on contas de gestão. Complements `Tema157`.

Combined effect: TCE rulings on contas de gestão have real sanctioning
bite (financial, administrative) but do not make a mayor ineligible.
Ineligibility flows only through the câmara vote on contas de governo.

## 3. Lei da Ficha Limpa — ineligibility from rejected accounts

`LI.1.I.g` (as amended by `LFL`). All four conditions must be met
simultaneously for 8-year ineligibility:

1. Rejection of accounts related to the exercise of public office.
2. Existence of an **irregularidade insanável** (the flaw cannot be
   cured ex post).
3. The irregularity configures **ato doloso de improbidade
   administrativa**.
4. **Decisão irrecorrível do órgão competente**, not suspended or
   annulled by the Judiciary.

"Órgão competente" for contas de prefeito = câmara municipal
(`Tema157`). TCE parecer alone does not suffice; neither does câmara
omission. Many rejections do not generate ineligibility because the
irregularity is deemed curable, non-dolous, or remains under judicial
appeal. ([TSE source](https://www.tse.jus.br/institucional/escola-judiciaria-eleitoral/publicacoes/revistas-da-eje/artigos/revista-eletronica-eje-n.-4-ano-3/inelegibilidade-por-rejeicao-de-contas-orgao-competente-para-julgar-as-contas-de-prefeito))

## 4. Voting modality in câmaras municipais

No federal mandate for open or secret voting on contas de prefeito.
Each municipality regulates this through its Lei Orgânica and
Regimento Interno. Constitutional default is publicity
(`CF.37.caput`): secret voting requires explicit constitutional
authorization, and at the municipal level there is none. Despite
this, many câmaras voted contas de prefeito by secret ballot through
the 2010s, on the strength of pre-1988 Lei Orgânica clauses that
were never updated.

### `EC76-2013` — federal precedent

Promulgated 2013-11-28. Abolished secret voting in Congress for two
acts: `CF.55` (cassação — secret to open) and `CF.66.§4`
(presidential vetoes — secret to open). Catalyzed by the Natan
Donadon case (August 2013), where deputies in secret ballot upheld
the mandate of a congressman sentenced to 13 years for embezzlement.
Not directly binding on câmaras municipais, but served as the
rhetorical catalyst for municipal reforms, which routinely cite it
alongside `CF.37.caput`.

### Municipal reform — diffusion, not coordination

Two-decade diffusion process, not a coordinated event. Examples from
ad-hoc review of câmara records (**illustrative, not a systematic
census**):

- **Early movers**: Criciúma/SC (2004, RI Art. 180), Canelinha/SC
  (2008, Emenda LOM nº 9), Belo Horizonte/MG (2012).
- **Post-EC 76/2013 acceleration (~2019–2021)**: Morro da Fumaça/SC,
  Ilhota/SC, Ibicaré/SC; Recife/PE; Pedralva/MG; São Simão/GO;
  Marechal Cândido Rondon/PR; Guarapari/ES; Uruguaiana/RS.
- **Late movers**: Capinzal/SC (2025).

### Practice vs. legal rule

Even where the Lei Orgânica prescribes nominal roll call, actual
practice may diverge (show of hands, voice votes). In small câmaras
even formally-secret ballots are de facto open. The legal rule is a
noisy proxy for actual observability.

## 5. `LRF` — load-bearing thresholds

The `LRF` (LC 101/2000) is the substantive backdrop against which
most adverse TCE pareceres are written. For the LRF as a federalism
instrument and the broader transfer/cap framework, see
`federalismo-fiscal.md`. Structural numbers for mayoral accounts:

- **Personnel spending caps** (`LRF.19`/`LRF.20`): municipalities
  capped at **60%** of net current revenue for total personnel, of
  which up to **6%** is reserved for the câmara. By far the most
  common ground for adverse TCE pareceres on contas de governo.
- **TCE parecer deadline** (`LRF.56`/`LRF.57`): parecer prévio
  within **60 days** of receipt; municipalities under 200k population
  may be granted **180 days**. Accounts must be published and made
  available for public consultation.
- **Alert mechanism** (`LRF.59`): TCEs must alert branches when
  spending approaches the LRF limits, before the breach is
  consummated. The operational tripwire that converts a fiscal
  trajectory into a documentary record the TCE relator can later
  cite.

## 6. Câmara procedure — the 2/3 rule and its asymmetry

Standard câmara procedure (committee parecer, plenary discussion,
vote, decreto legislativo) is not specific to contas. What is
load-bearing is the quorum structure of `CF.31.§2`.

### The 2/3 rule is asymmetric

| TCE recommends | Câmara wants to | Quorum needed |
|---|---|---|
| Reject | Approve (override) | **2/3 of members** |
| Approve | Reject (override) | **2/3 of members** |
| Either | Follow the TCE | Simple majority |

Following the TCE is "free" (simple majority); overriding requires a
supermajority. Strong institutional thumb on the scale for the TCE
recommendation regardless of direction. Empirically, the câmara vote
is informative mainly when it diverges — divergence is rare and
concentrated where 2/3 of councillors share an interest.

### Omission as a strategy

Some câmaras never vote on pending accounts, sometimes for years.
Per `Tema157`, omission does **not** generate Ficha Limpa
ineligibility. Omission is therefore a deliberate strategic
equilibrium: no rejection (no ineligibility for an allied mayor), no
explicit approval (no political cost). The share of
pending-but-unvoted accounts is a useful outcome variable — it tracks
câmara-mayor alignment more cleanly than the rejection rate.

## 7. Timeline of key institutional changes

| Date | Event | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| 1988 | CF/1988 promulgated | `CF.31.§2` establishes parecer prévio / 2/3 override |
| 2000 | `LRF` (LC 101/2000) | Fiscal rules whose violation generates TCE rejections |
| 2010 | `LFL` (LC 135/2010, Ficha Limpa) | Strengthens ineligibility consequences |
| 2013-11-28 | `EC76-2013` | Open voting in Congress for cassação and vetoes; rhetorical catalyst for municipal reforms |
| 2016-08-10 | STF `Tema157` decided | Câmara is the competent organ; TCE parecer alone doesn't trigger ineligibility |
| 2019-10-18 | `Tema157` thesis certified | Binding precedent formally established |
| 2025 | `ADPF982` decided | TCEs can independently judge contas de gestão; only câmara rejection of contas de governo triggers Ficha Limpa |
